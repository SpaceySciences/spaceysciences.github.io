Caleb Adams


Caleb doesn’t like the idea of titles in a startup, but if he had one it would be CEO. Caleb brings with him to the company the experience of having founded the Small Satellite Research Lab at the University of Georgia with Graham Grable and Hollis Neel. It’s through this organization that Caleb leads a team of fifty plus building satellites for NASA and the DOD Caleb was one of the original members of the Hackathon team who snuck with press badges into the Virginia Tech conference and won the event.

Hollis Neel


Hollis Neel brings mathematics and programming expertise to the team. Nicholas Neel is a co-founder of The University of Georgia’s Small Satellite Research Lab where he works on the initial algorithmic tests to determine the feasibility of passive satellite mapping using structure from motion in low earth orbit. He developed and printed one of the largest 3D printed, remote operable telescopes in the world which has since become the basis for the company’s product designs.

Graham Grable


Graham Grable serves as the Chief Engineer of the company. As a part of the University of Georgia’s engineering school, Graham uses his training as a mechanical engineer to design and build the company’s products. Graham helped to get UGA’s Small Satellite Research Laboratory off the ground, alongside Caleb Adams and Nicholas Neel, leading the mechanical team for the lab’s missions.

Ethan Bilkert


Ethan Bilkert serves a variety of roles in the company, but most notably he manages the company’s finances. He also helps with marketing, sales, and operations alongside the other founders bringing with him a passion for entrepreneurship and startup experience. Ethan earned his bachelor’s and master’s in accounting from the University of Georgia and ran a mobile boat detailing startup on Lake Oconee while in school.
